ADHD Comminucation

  1. Active Listening: Focus, maintain eye contact, and summarize key points for understanding during conversations.

  2. Clarity and Brevity: Keep messages simple to aid comprehension, avoiding complexity in explanations.

  3. Visual Tools: Use diagrams or charts to supplement verbal communication for better understanding.

  4. Structured Instructions: Break tasks into manageable steps to prevent overwhelming listeners.

  5. Encourage Feedback: Create an open environment for questions or thoughts to clarify understanding.

  6. Minimize Distractions: Engage in conversations in quiet, low-distraction settings.

  7. Positive Reinforcement: Acknowledge and reinforce good communication habits.

  8. Establish Communication Norms: Set clear expectations for effective communication.

  9. Patience and Flexibility: Allow time for processing information and respond with patience and flexibility.

  10. Professional Support: Seek guidance from specialists to enhance communication skills.
